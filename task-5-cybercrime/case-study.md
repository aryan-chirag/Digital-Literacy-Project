# 💳 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## 📌 Introduction
UPI has made digital payments quick and convenient, but it has also increased the risk of online fraud. UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India.

## 🔍 What is UPI Fraud?
- UPI fraud is a cybercrime where users are tricked into sending money or sharing sensitive details.  
- It involves misuse of OTPs, UPI PINs, or fake payment requests.  

## ⚙️ How Does It Happen? (Step-by-Step)
- Scammers contact victims via calls, messages, or social media. 📞  
- They pretend to be bank officials, customer support, or buyers/sellers.  
- They send fake payment requests, links, or QR codes.  
- Victims are asked to approve requests or share OTP/PIN.  
- Money gets deducted instead of being received.  

## 🧾 Example Case
- A student named Rahul wanted to sell his old phone online.  
- A fake buyer contacted him and sent a “payment request” link.  
- The buyer told him to approve it to receive money.  
- Rahul approved the request and entered his UPI PIN.  
- Instead of receiving money, ₹5,000 was deducted from his account.  

## 🎯 Who Are the Targets?
- Students  
- Elderly individuals  
- People unfamiliar with digital payments  

## ⚠️ Consequences
- Financial loss  
- Mental stress  
- Risk of identity theft  

## 🔒 Conclusion
UPI fraud highlights the need for digital awareness. Users should never share OTPs or PINs, must verify payment requests, and avoid suspicious links. Staying alert is the best way to stay safe.
